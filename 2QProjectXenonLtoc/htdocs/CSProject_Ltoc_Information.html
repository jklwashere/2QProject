Video of the Month

"Rising Tides: Understanding Sea Level Rise" by NASA Climate Change

As you can see, the featured video for January 2021 is a short animation video by NASA Climate Change explaining the rise of sea levels and its possible outcomes. As global temperature keeps increasing, ice sheets all around the world start deteriorating and sea levels keeps rising. This is bad news for a lot of people, especially living in heavily urbanized cities on the coast such as Jakarta, Dhaka, Mumbai, and Miami. Some nations are even at risk at sinking completely, such as the Maldives, Kiribati, and the Marshall Islands.


According to climate.gov, ocean levels have risen around 21 to 24 centimeters since 1880. The rate sea levels have also increased dramatically from around 1.4 millimeters in the 20th century to around 3.3-3.6 millimeters this decade. At the end of this cenutry, it is looking pretty grim as estimates show that by then, sea levels have risen by 0.3-2.5 meters (1-8 feet). This is why helping prevent further global warming is an important responsibility of every government in the world, as most nations can't afford to lose a lot of their land to the rising oceans.
