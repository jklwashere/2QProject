Projects to stop Desertification


Every year, the Sahara slowly expands across the African continent, and along with it affecting one of the poorest areas of the planet, the Sahel. However, there have been massive efforts to stop the Sahara's expansion, the most notable effort being the Great Green Wall, where multiple countries in the Sahelian and Sub-Sahelian region have cooperated together in planting millions of trees to prevent further desertification.

Pacific Islands' Mangrove Efforts


As ocean levels continue to rise, there have been multiple projects to determine how to prevent the further rise of sea levels or at the very least, how to slow it down for now. Pacific Island nations such as Kiribati, the Marshall Islands, and Tuvalu find themselves rapidly sinking, and thus they've turned to mangrove forests to prevent further erosion and sinking of their islands, as mangrove forests act as natural barriers against the rising water.
